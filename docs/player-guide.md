# Player Guide

This guide explains the game from the player's perspective.

## The Story

You run Shree Shyam Bhandar, a small kirana store, for 30 days. Every day you decide what to stock, what to discount, which customers to follow up with, and whether to market the shop.

Your goal is not just to earn money. You need to balance:

- cash in hand
- customer trust
- stock availability
- perishable waste
- khata collection
- marketing quality
- day-by-day learning

## Starting A Game

The first screen asks for a player name.

That name creates or restores a saved player session. If two players enter different names, their games are kept separate. If the same player enters the same name again, their saved runs are visible to that player.

After login, the first decision is opening stock. The shop starts with empty shelves and ₹3,000.

## First Decision: Buy Opening Stock

Before Day 1 customers arrive, decide what goes on the shelf.

Good opening stock usually needs a mix of:

- essentials: milk, bread, eggs, Maggi
- quick-sale items: chips, cold drinks
- perishables: bananas, milk, bread
- cash reserve for Day 2 correction

Do not spend all cash blindly. If Day 1 reveals missed demand, you need cash to correct it.

## Main Screen Flow

The game tries to keep one clear mental model:

```text
Read signals -> inspect inventory -> choose actions -> simulate day -> review results
```

### 1. Insights

Insights show what is happening around the shop:

- weekday and date
- weather forecast
- customer rhythm
- market pressure
- shop memory from yesterday
- AI-generated neighborhood context when configured

Use this section to reason about tomorrow. The game intentionally avoids telling you exact future demand for every product. Instead, it gives environmental clues so your decision feels like shopkeeping, not filling a spreadsheet.

### 2. Inventory Row

The item row shows the current stock situation by SKU. Each item card indicates health using color, count, sold units, missed units, and perishability.

Click an item to open the item popup.

### 3. Item Popup

The item popup is the main place for item-level decisions.

It shows:

- item margin
- sold units
- missed demand
- closing stock
- recent day history
- opening stock per day
- customer demand per day
- waste
- order quantity
- discard quantity
- offer/discount choice
- wholesaler cart save state

Use the popup to decide if the item needs restocking, removal, or an offer.

### 4. Marketing Selector

Marketing appears below the inventory row. Campaigns cost money and create demand pressure for specific customer segments and product groups.

Marketing is not free score. A campaign scores well only when promoted demand is actually served profitably.

### 5. Floating Decision Bar

The bottom decision bar summarizes the current plan:

- order cost
- cash after plan
- selected marketing
- offers/removals/khata reminders

The Open Next Day button is separate from item ordering. Save item edits into the wholesaler cart first, then open the next day when the whole plan is ready.

## Day Simulation

When the day runs, the game shows a fast-forward shopping experience. The live scoreboard updates:

- visits
- items sold
- revenue
- missed units
- khata
- stockouts
- cash
- score

This connects your plan to the simulated day instead of hiding all consequences until the report.

## Evening Report

After simulation, the report explains what happened.

Important areas:

- scorecards for money, inventory, visits, and rewards
- opening and closing inventory movement
- customer demand ledger
- item-level trends
- customer fulfillment and khata behavior
- reward breakdown

The best way to read the report:

1. Check score and cash.
2. Check missed units and stockouts.
3. Check which items caused the misses.
4. Check who was affected.
5. Plan tomorrow's correction.

## How To Think Like A Good Player

Good players do not just chase maximum stock. They ask:

- Which items are essential for trust?
- Which items are perishable?
- Which items have high margin?
- Which items missed demand today?
- Is tomorrow a hot day, rainy day, weekend, school day, or festival window?
- Do I have enough cash after ordering?
- Will marketing create demand I cannot serve?
- Are khata customers delaying my usable cash?

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Hurts |
| --- | --- |
| Spending all cash on Day 1 | No cash left to correct Day 2. |
| Overstocking perishables | Waste and risk cost hurt inventory score. |
| Ignoring milk/bread | Essentials stockouts damage trust. |
| Marketing without stock | Promoted stockouts hurt marketing score. |
| Treating khata as cash | Revenue on khata does not immediately improve cash. |
| Clicking Open Next Day before saving popup edits | Draft item changes may not be in the wholesaler cart. |

## Winning Mindset

The game rewards disciplined correction. You do not need to predict perfectly. You need to learn from yesterday, protect cash, serve important demand, and avoid creating avoidable problems.
